Tech & Meet Events

This page gives an overview of the Tech & Meet sessions I attended as part of my Professional Networking portfolio. Each event helped me explore a different area of IT, cybersecurity, networking, or software development.

Flutter Tech & Meet

A session about Flutter, cross-platform development, widgets, performance and the importance of developer communities.

Reflection: Flutter supports mobile, web, desktop and embedded development. It is built around widgets and makes it easier to create apps across platforms. The session was useful because understanding development frameworks helps me better understand the environments I may need to secure in the future.

.NET 10 Demystified

A session about the new developments in .NET 10, including runtime improvements, C# updates and cloud-native development.

Reflection: I attended a Tech & Meet session called ".NET 10 Demystified", presented by Kevin De Rudder. The session focused on the improvements and new possibilities introduced with .NET 10, including performance, tooling, libraries, C# improvements and cloud-native development. It broadened my view of software development and showed how security and development are connected.

IPv6 Migration

A networking-focused session about IPv4, NAT, IPv6 adoption, dual-stack networks and IPv6-mostly environments.

Reflection: IPv4 survived longer than expected because of NAT, but IPv6 adoption is becoming increasingly necessary. The session explained Dual Stack and IPv6-mostly setups and reinforced that security professionals need to understand modern networking developments.

NATO Cyber Defence

A cybersecurity session about cyber defence strategy, critical infrastructure, Zero Trust, risk management and modern threats.

Reflection: I attended a Tech & Meet session about the development of NATO's cyber defence capabilities. The talk connected technical threats with strategy, planning and collaboration. It also highlighted that cybersecurity is not a new challenge and that modern threats include advanced persistent threats and weaponised AI.
