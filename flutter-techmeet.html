This Tech & Meet session introduced me to Flutter through an engaging presentation by Thijs Pirmez, a self-taught developer whose enthusiasm for the technology was immediately noticeable. One of the aspects that stood out most was that Flutter’s success extends beyond the framework itself and is strongly influenced by the community surrounding it. It was interesting to see how open collaboration and community contributions continue to shape the ecosystem. During the session, I learned that Flutter supports multiple platforms including mobile, web, desktop and embedded systems, making it an attractive option for developers aiming to build applications efficiently across environments. I was also introduced to the concept that everything in Flutter is built using widgets, which changes the way interfaces are structured and managed. Topics such as Flutter Flame and Jaspr showed that Flutter extends beyond traditional application development into areas I had not previously explored. Although software development is not my primary focus within cybersecurity, attending this session broadened my understanding of modern application ecosystems and reminded me that security professionals also benefit from understanding how applications are built. Overall, this event demonstrated the importance of staying curious and continuously learning outside my immediate specialisation. Would I attend again? Yes. The session made technical concepts approachable and encouraged exploration of technologies outside my normal focus.
